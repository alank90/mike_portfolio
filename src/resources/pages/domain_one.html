Domain I: Planning and Preparation


I consider planning to be an essential part of my responsibilities as a teacher, and I put considerable effort into taking the curriculum and making it accessible and interesting to our students. Two key aspects of my work in this area include having and developing a deep knowledge of the subject matter as well as a strong understanding of the diverse learners that are in my classroom. I design my instruction and assessments in light of these two aspects.

Knowledge of Students

Student Questionnaire

I give my students a short questionnaire at the start of the year as their first homework assignment. The questionnaire helps me to know their academic and extracurricular interests and establish a connection with them in a context outside of the mathematics or engineering classroom. A copy of the questionnaire can be found here.

Office Hours

I use office hours and free periods as a key way to get to know my students and build relationships with them. Being in a more informal environment than the classroom helps students to open up and also allows me to have conversations with them and provide mentorship to them outside of the subject matter that I teach in class.


Designing Coherent Instruction

Rational Exponents and Kepler's Third Law of Planetary Motion (Math 433)

Below is my lesson plan for an Algebra class on the topic of rational exponents. The student handout begins with a "Do Now" section in which the students warm up with some problems whose solutions were studied earlier in the year. These problems motivate the discussion of rational exponents. This is followed by some exercises for them to complete on the topic of the lesson. Finally, there is an application problem for them to see how the mathematical theory can be used in a real-world context.
